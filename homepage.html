About Palestine

Palestinian culture is a diverse and rich blend of Arab, Islamic, and Mediterranean influences.
The culture has been shaped by the country's long history, its geography, and the traditions of its people.
Palestinian culture encompasses music, dance, food, art, and literature, all of which reflect the country's
complex history and its present-day struggles. Traditional Palestinian dress is often brightly colored and
embroidered with intricate designs, and traditional Palestinian music features instruments such as the oud,
qanun, and darbuka. Palestinian cuisine is renowned for its use of fresh herbs and spices, and dishes such as
falafel, hummus, and shawarma have become popular around the world. Despite the challenges faced by the Palestinian
people, their culture remains a vibrant and important part of the region's history and identity.
